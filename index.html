Event: Engine failure during flight leading to a crash

Severity Level:

  • Physical Injury: An engine failure during flight can lead to a crash, which could result in multiple fatalities. This would be rated as "Multiple Fatality" (A).
  • Damage to Assets or Potential Loss of Revenue: The loss of the aircraft would be catastrophic, and the revenue loss (from lawsuits, increased insurance premiums, grounded flights, etc.) could be immense. This would also be rated as "Catastrophic Damage" (A).
  • Damage to Reputation: Such an incident would be highly publicized and could cause significant damage to the airline's reputation, potentially leading to a loss of customers. This would be rated as "Catastrophic Impact" (A).
  • Damage to the Environment: Depending on where the crash occurred, there could be significant environmental damage, such as fuel leaks causing pollution. This would likely be rated as "Major Effect" (B) or "Massive Effect" (A), depending on the specifics.

Likelihood Level:

  • Unknown but Possible Event in Aviation: Engine failures during flight, while rare, are known to occur in the aviation industry. Therefore, this would not be a "1".
  • Known in Aviation Industry: Engine failures are known in the aviation industry, so this would be a "2".
  • Occurred in Biz Aviation: If there have been occurrences in business aviation, it would be a "3".
  • CIA Framework Blockage: If such an incident has led to a blockage in the CIA Framework (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability), it would be a "4".
  • Occurred in the CIA: If such incidents have already occurred in the CIA, it would be a "5".

The exact classification would again depend on the specific circumstances of the aircraft and the incident. However, for this example, we might say the event is at Severity Level A (due to the potential for multiple fatalities and catastrophic damage) and Likelihood Level 3 (if this kind of event has occurred in business aviation before). Therefore, the event would be classified as "3A".

Risk Assessment: Wet Runway Landing with Tailwind

For the Severity Level, we might consider:

  • Physical Injury: A hard landing or a runway excursion due to the wet surface and tailwind could potentially result in minor to serious injuries, but it is not likely to lead to fatalities if managed properly. This would be rated as "Minor Injury" (D) to "Serious Injury" (C).
  • Damage to Assets or Potential Loss of Revenue: A runway excursion or hard landing could lead to substantial damage to the aircraft, and downtime for repairs would lead to a potential loss of revenue. This would be rated as "Substantial Damage" (C).
  • Damage to Reputation: Such an event could cause some damage to the airline's reputation, as passengers might feel unsafe. However, if handled properly, the long-term impact should be limited. This would be rated as "Limited Impact" (C).
  • Damage to the Environment: An incident on a wet runway is unlikely to cause significant environmental damage unless there is a fuel leak or other hazardous material release, which is less likely in this scenario. This would be rated as "No Effect" (E) to "Minor Effect" (D).

For the Likelihood Level, we would consider:

  • Unknown but Possible Event in Aviation: Landings on wet runways with tailwinds, while challenging, are a known potential scenario in aviation. Therefore, this would not be a "1".
  • Known in Aviation Industry: These types of landings are known in the aviation industry, so this would be a "2".
